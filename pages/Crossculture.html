Bissky on Disagreement by raising objections

How do you communicate disagreement? What does the other party understand you to mean, when you raise objections to their plan? How do you say 'No' in English?

Greg Bissky says Westerners and Chinese business people may fail to communicate because of their misunderstanding of what the other party believes objections mean, in Western-Chinese Business: Saying No Without Saying No on Youtube.

Logging in

Note: You need to login to transcribe the conversation.

  • Your name is your Chinese name in Chinese characters. Your ID is your school ID.
  • The first letter of the ID, eg U or N, is a capital (big) letter.
  • Your password is the first half of your first name. It is in English, not the Chinese character. Thus JiaQi's pasword is Jia. See Lin YuTang's Chinese-English dictionary for the spelling.
  • If I was not able to find the first character in your name, your password may be a question mark. Eg, 賴?岑. Other people whose names my computer does not have Chinese characters for are 黃慧? and ?琮婷, but their passwords should be okay.
