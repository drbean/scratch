Business English--Listening

Business English students. Each week, each member in a group has a different sound track to listen to. Depending on whether you are A, B, C or D, I want you to listen to a different soundfile, After listening to your sound track, tell your partners about it. How is the information on your track different/similar to the information on their tracks? Discuss what you think of the situation that all the tracks are about.
The sound files were not recorded by real people. They were produced by the Festival text-to-speech system, from texts Dr Bean wrote based on each chapter of Market Leader, the textbook.
We will work on one chapter over two weeks. So that means we will be doing two sets of homework based on each chapter.

Getting on top of work

Work is something that is hard to avoid, but which can destroy you. How do you take control of work, and not let work take control of you?

Thinking about your career--Would you be a good candidate?

This week we are doing exams about your career and being a job candidate. Why will the person who has thought about his/her career be the best candidate?

Only one track this week, the connection between thinking about your career and being a job candidate. Think about the career tips on page 8. What would the candidates on page 13 choose as most important?

Fill in the blanks in the careercandidate exercise. (You will be asked to log in.)
