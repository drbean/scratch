Self-Access Listening

Links and commentary about sound files on the Internet. Listen to the soundfiles and write down what you hear.

Business English--Listening

Business English students. Each week, each member in a group has a different sound track to listen to. Depending on whether you are A, B, C or D, I want you to listen to a different soundfile, After listening to your sound track, tell your partners about it. How is the information on your track different/similar to the information on their tracks? Discuss what you think of the situation that all the tracks are about.
The sound files were not recorded by real people. They were produced by the Festival text-to-speech system, from texts Dr Bean wrote based on each chapter of Market Leader, the textbook.
We will work on one chapter over two weeks. So that means we will be doing two sets of homework based on each chapter.

Getting on top of work

Work is something that is hard to avoid, but which can destroy you. How do you take control of work, and not let work take control of you?

Week 1: How to get ahead at work

Look at the 10 ways of thinking about your career in Reading B on page 8 of Market Leader. These are all important things to do if you want to make something of your life. Which are the most important ideas?

All 4 people, AWB, BDL, SLT, and KED have different ideas of what's important when thinking about your career. But they each chose 3 of the 10 ways in the book. Which ways did they choose? Two people each chose one idea which was the same.

  • A students: Listen to AWB talk about planning, networking and family.
  • B students: Listen to BDL say you need to think about the job and try to make it better.
  • C students: Listen to SLT talk about teamwork, doing things the hard way and being organized.
  • D students: Listen to KED suggest you need to keep learning, make sure the boss understands how you think, and think about what you are doing.

Fill in the blanks in the career exercise. (You will be asked to log in.)
