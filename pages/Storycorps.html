American Stories at Storycorps

Storycorps is an American project to record the stories of everyday Americans. You can hear them each Friday morning on NPR radio.
A lot of the stories are not difficult to understand. So they are suitable for people learning English. At the same time, they are real stories, moving, interesting and likeable.

Mixed-message marriage stories

Rebia and Frank

Listen to Rebia Mixon-Clay talking about her late ex-husband, Frank Mixon: You're going to be my wife.

Frank and Rebia separated and Frank was killed helping a woman. She mentions an altercation and a damsel in distress. See the Wikipedia entry.

Frank wanted to give her his class ring, but she didn't want it. Do high school and college students, or military officers wear class rings in Taiwan?

Funny (??) divorce story

Marriage not always pretty

A mother tells her 11-year-old son about meeting his dad when she was 18. A romantic start to a 23-year relationship, but their marriage has not always been good. They were separated for 2 years, and she thought of getting divorced. Read the NPR write-up.
