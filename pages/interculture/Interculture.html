Multilingualism and Multiculturalism

Multilingualism in Switzerland

There are four official languages in Switzerland. But most people only speak one of those languages. The most common second language they speak is English!!! That's like in Taiwan, Mandarin doesn't exist, everyone speaks either Taiwanese or Hakka, and the only other language they speak is some English. Can you imagine that?

Listen to this video recording from a French-speaking Swiss woman, Stephanie Booth on multilingualism in Switzerland.

Bilingual education in San Francisco's Chinatown

Nichols vs Lau, a very famous law case in 1974 introduced bilingual education to the US. Now however, California schools are not allowed to teach students in their first languages for more than one year. Why? What do you think about these changes in bilingual education?

Bissky on questions and expressing disagreement

Do you like to ask questions in front of a lot of your peers? Do you like to express your disagreement when you don't agree with someone?

Look at Bissky talk about how Western business people find doing business in China frustrating because of the Chinese reluctance to ask questions and express disagreement, in Western-Chinese Business: Different Rules of Communication on Youtube.

This is a long listening passage. Did you get his four rules.
  • His Western rule about asking questions.
  • His Chinese rule about not asking questions.
  • His rule about not doing anything in groups in China.
  • His Western rule about stating honest opinions.

He implies that Chinese do not state their honest opinion, and they also do not express disagreement honestly. What do you think? I don't think what he says is really correct.

Bissky on Disagreement by raising objections

How do you communicate disagreement? What does the other party understand you to mean, when you raise objections to their plan?

Greg Bissky says Westerners and Chinese business people may fail to communicate because of their misunderstanding of what the other party believes objections mean, in Western-Chinese Business: Saying No Without Saying No on Youtube.
